Help us secure a dedicated volleyball court

Our Malvern court was demolished without notice after 30+ years of community use. Add your name to demand accountability from the City — and a permanent home for volleyball in our community.

What happened

30+ years of history, removed overnight

For over three decades, the Malvern volleyball court was a gathering place for our community — a space that gave new immigrants their first friendships and sense of belonging, and stood strong even through the difficult years of the 1990s.

The court was demolished without advance notice or consultation, and converted into a pickleball court. A similar loss happened previously at what is now Ali Abbas Park (formerly McLevin Park). If the City can find space for pickleball, it can find space for the sport that has served our community for over 30 years.

Our demands

What we're asking the City for

01
Full accountability

An explanation for why no advance notice was given before demolition, and why consultation was bypassed.

02
Mandatory consultation going forward

A formal commitment to consult active community users before changing any public recreational space.

03
A dedicated volleyball court

A permanent, accessible space for volleyball — open to our community and every other group that loves the sport.

04
Action from our elected representatives

Our community holds real civic voice. We expect that to translate into active advocacy for this space.

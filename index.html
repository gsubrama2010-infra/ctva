About us

Built by the community,
for the community

The Canadian Tamil Volleyball Association began as an informal gathering of newcomer families on a single court in Malvern. Over more than three decades, it grew into a network of 18 clubs across the Greater Toronto Area — each with its own name, colours, and following, all connected by the same court culture.

Through the 1990s, our courts were a place of safety during difficult years for the neighbourhood. For new immigrants arriving with little else, they became the first place to make friends, find community, and feel at home in a new country.

Today CTVA represents that entire network — coordinating tournaments, preserving our history, and now, advocating for the permanent facilities our community has earned.

Our mission

Growing the sport we grew up with

CTVA exists to organize, develop, and grow volleyball within our community — running leagues and tournaments, supporting our 18 member clubs, training new players, and keeping the sport accessible to every generation that wants to play.

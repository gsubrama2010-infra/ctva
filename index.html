About us

Built by the community,
for the community

The Canadian Tamil Volleyball Association began as an informal gathering of newcomer families throughout Scarborough. Over more than three decades, it grew into a network of 20+ clubs across the Greater Toronto Area, Montreal, Ottawa, and Vancouver — each with its own name, colours, and following, all connected by the same volleyball culture.

Through the 1990s, our courts were a place of safety during difficult years for the neighbourhood. For new immigrants arriving with little else, they became the first place to make friends, find community, and feel at home in a new country.

Today CTVA represents that entire network — coordinating tournaments, preserving our history, and now, advocating for the permanent facilities our community has earned.

01

A distinct version of the sport

Tamil volleyball follows its own set of rules, ball, and net requirements, and isn't played alongside the traditional game — though it uses the same court and equipment.

02

Open to all

While rooted in Tamil-Canadian history, our courts have always welcomed every background that loves the sport.

03

Run democratically

CTVA is governed by committees and a voting system, by and for its member clubs.

Our mission

Growing the sport we grew up with

CTVA exists to organize, develop, and grow volleyball within our community — running leagues and tournaments, supporting our 20+ member clubs, training new players, and keeping the sport accessible to every generation that wants to play.

About us

Built by the community,
for the community

The Canadian Tamil Volleyball Association began as an informal gathering of newcomer families throughout Scarborough. Over more than three decades, it grew into a network of 29 clubs across the Greater Toronto Area, Montreal, Ottawa, and Vancouver.

Through the 1990s, our courts were a place of safety during difficult years. For new immigrants arriving with little else, they became the first place to make friends and feel at home in a new country.

Today CTVA coordinates tournaments, preserves our history, and advocates for the permanent facilities our community has earned.

01

A distinct version of the sport

Tamil volleyball follows its own rules, ball, and net requirements — distinct from the traditional game.

02

Open to all

While rooted in Tamil-Canadian history, our courts have always welcomed every background.

03

Run democratically

CTVA is governed by committees and a voting system, by and for its member clubs.

Mahinthan

A devoted player, a trusted friend, and a pillar of this community. Mahinthan played with his whole heart — on the court and off it — right up until his sudden passing. His energy, his laugh, and his love for this sport live on in every game we play.
